The Pilot G2

America's Champion of Writing

✒️
What ties together a man named Lazlo Biro, advanced metallurgy, and the United States' Declaration of Independence? These are credited as the maker, the method, and arguably the masterwork of the same engineering marvel: the pen. A great deal of human history and literature has been captured in the form of writing. The latest member in an evolutionary line of carvings, brushes, and quills of the Revolution is the ballpoint pen. Amongst these pens, there is a champion in the United States: The Pilot G2. It is the nation's most popular pen, seemingly bridging the entire American experience through art, science, and diplomacy on a global scale.

A Journey Through Time

~1000 BCE

The writing brush emerges in China, considered by many as the earliest ancestor of the modern pen. An ancient tool that would inspire millennia of innovation.

Early 20th Century

Lazlo Biro, the father of the modern ballpoint pen, revolutionizes writing. Frustrated with leaking fountain pens, he replaced the nib with a freely-spinning metal ball that drew ink from a reservoir above.

Mid-20th Century

The "biro" spreads like wildfire across the western world. Stationery begins to evolve at a far faster pace than ever before, with improvements in inks, ergonomics, and customization.

Modern Era

The Japan-based Pilot Pen company creates the Pilot G2 gel pen, building on decades of innovation to become America's #1 Pen.

Engineering Marvel

The Pilot G2, and indeed all ballpoint pens, are a gift to society, driving innovation through their production and applications. When designing the pen, Biro ran into two major hurdles: the ball and the ink.

The pen's invention represented new applications of technology that was already revolutionizing the world at the time.

The Ball

Made of immensely strong materials like tungsten carbide, the ball is ground down and polished to the perfect size. The same metallurgical processes were used to create ball bearings for heavy machinery.

The Ink

Oil-based ink took countless iterations to ensure it dried on paper but not in the reservoir, while maintaining the perfect viscosity. Drawing inspiration from newspaper printing ink, this chemistry-driven iteration was applied to medicines and industrial lubricants.

Modern Innovation

Powder metallurgy creates even finer, higher quality ball bearings by "precisely controlling sintering temperature and powder particle size characteristics to achieve high density, hardness, and strength."

The G2 Evolution

The Pilot G2 uses cutting-edge ball bearings while evolving beyond Biro's formula, employing a smoother, water-based gel ink for an unparalleled writing experience.

A Legacy of Innovation

Even beyond the fact that writing with pens has become the language of modern signatures and contracts, the wave of engineering innovation has continued.

These pens have driven invention for over a century and continue to do so.
